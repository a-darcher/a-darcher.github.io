hello 🌻

I study how human brains extract information from complex and naturalistic content.

I'm a PhD candidate co-supervised by Florian Mormann at the Universitätsklinikum Bonn and Jakob Macke at the University of Tübingen. My work focuses on how single neurons in the human hippocampus, amygdala, and neighboring regions represent semantic content, such as people, places, and objects. In particular, I study how single neurons develop such representations, and how these representations are affected by the complexity of the stimulus presentation.

Prior to my PhD studies, I adapted meditation practices to treat symptoms of social isolation and depression in at-risk patient groups.
